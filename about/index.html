History Of College

GIET College Campus

Godavari Institute of Engineering & Technology (GIET) was established in 1998 to offer engineering education of world-class standards. Over the years, the college has come a long way to gain a place of repute and a preferred destination for quality engineering education. Located in the serene and sylvan suburbs of Chaitanya Nagar, Rajahmundry on NH-16, the sprawling 300-acre campus of GIET campus reflects the finest educational facilities around. The stately 5-storeyed structure set amid idyllic setting ensures a most congenial atmosphere for scholastic pursuit in right earnest. An ISO-certified,NBA-accredited and NAAC-A Grade accredited institution, GIET has many distinctions to its credit.

Useful list:

  • First and the only Apple Centre of Excellence in the region
  • Only engineering college in the region with the Centre for Red Hat Learning offering training for RHCP Certification
  • Only engineering college in the region to have alliance with Oracle Corporation for Oracle Certification Courses
  • Only engineering college around implementing practice-oriented pedagogy Satyam Adhyayan module in alliance with Satyam Computer Services
  • Only engineering college around to have TCS Accreditation

Chairman Message

Dr. KVV Satyanarayana Raju, Founder and Chairman

Founder & Chairman, Chaitanya Group Torpedoed by the tides of time, today the firmament of higher education is in agreat ferment. In the wake of intense shake-up triggered by the hidden fault-lines, many engineering colleges and institutions offering professional courses have been swept of their feet in recent times. Even more are expected to follow suit.Proliferation has given way to purgation. Few could come to terms unscathed. Fewer could even think of consolidation.

Principal Message

Dr. D.V.Rama murthy

“Education is what remains after one has forgotten everything learned” —Albert Einstein When one forgets everything taught in class-rooms, something must remain ingrained in the self if one is to be called “educated.” That something comprises of a few ingredients: common sense, curiosity, confidence, civic sense, compassion, and courage. If these qualities are present, all that is forgotten resurfaces from the subconscious to make the person most erudite in his field of activity. We will aspire that our students become excellent learners, excellent thinkers, excellent reformers, and excellent leaders.

Managing Director

K. Ravi Kiran Varma

At Godavari Institute of Engineering & Technology (GIET), we are driven by just one consideration: “excellence in everything we do.” Whatever the costs and whatever the efforts required towards that end, we are simply unremitting. The features and facilities at GIET are proof enough. We have built up the very best of infrastructure. We pooled up the best of faculty and facilities. We have promoted a consummate world-class learning environment. We have forged purposive alliances with the corporate world. That’s what enables us to produce outstanding technical talent. That’s what attracts several top-league corporate houses to the campus scouting of promising engineering talent.It will remain our commitment and constant endeavour to keep continuously shoring up our competences and resources, so that we can offer students the very best of engineering education.

Exicutive Director,Chaitanya Group

K. Sasi Kiran Varma

" Transmitting knowledge is the easiest part of teaching. But, far more challenging is the task of equipping next generation of would-be engineers with the skills that they will need for real and lasting professional success. It is becoming increasingly important that engineers be innovative, entrepreneurial, collaborative and able to work globally. At GIET College of Engineering, our instructional design processes are aligned to that all-important goal. Our pedagogy practices focus on fostering a set of learning experiences that allow students primarily to develop a sound knowledge of disciplinary fundamentals, creative thinking and problem-solving skills together with the ability to apply theory in practice. We are relentless in our ambition to push the boundaries of engineering education.. We are constantly re-examining and reengineering our educational offerings - both in-class learning and out-of-class experiences - to adapt them to meeting future needs."

K Sasi Kiran Varma
Vice-Chairman, GIET
